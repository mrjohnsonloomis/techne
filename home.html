About the Symposium

A Gathering for Depth and Discussion

This symposium gathers a small group of interested collaborators from across secondary and higher education to spend three days in an intimate setting thinking about AI, writing, and teaching. Our theme is critical literacies — and we approach it from three distinct angles.

Three Strands

Our Approaches

I

The Great Books

A great books approach to the history of algorithms and artificial intelligence — tracing the ideas that have shaped how we think about intelligence, calculation, and the machine.

II

Writing-Based Teaching

Modeling writing-based teaching practices to consider the intersections of writing and technology — exploring what it means to think through and with language in an age of AI.

III

The Creative Laboratory

A human-centered, creative laboratory approach to AI — making, experimenting, and discovering what it means to collaborate with intelligent systems in imaginative and generative ways.

"Techne" — the ancient Greek word for craft, skill, and art. A reminder that teaching has always been a technology.
